## Friendship Philosophy

### Core Insight: You Cannot Buy Friendship

**The patron/recipient trap**: When you pay for everything, you become host/benefactor; others become guests/recipients. The equality friendship requires is structurally undermined.

**Clinical illustration**: Client with significant wealth who "can no longer" charter jets to Scottish castles. Those friends could never reciprocate. *She cannot now know which friendships would have existed without the jets.*

**Counter-illustration**: Client with five properties who belongs nowhere. Optionality prevented sustained presence.

### The Milestone Exception

Birthdays, anniversaries, significant life transitions: socially acceptable contexts for asymmetric hosting. The event is *about* the person celebrated. No ongoing expectation created.

### When Resources CAN Serve Friendship

**With an existing deep friendship**, paying for shared experiences is not patronage — it is accommodating a friend's circumstances.

**Resources as lubricant**: Concert tickets, Ubers home, phone calls, occasional catered parties. **The key is invisibility.** The resource disappears into the experience.

### The Formula for Belonging

**Belonging has a minimum dose.** Below that dose, you are a visitor everywhere.

**What creates community**:
- Regular presence over cycles of years
- Showing up to the same place, repeatedly
- Being available when moments of connection arise
- Not forcing it; letting it unfold

**What does NOT create community**: Funding events others attend. Maximising optionality. Waiting for perfect circumstances.

**The principle**: "First the root, then the flower."

### Availability Must Exceed Uptake

**Clinical origin**: Advice to parents — you have to be around for *twice as much opportunity as the child actually avails themselves of*.

**Application to friendship**: Connection cannot be scheduled. It happens in the surplus, in unforced moments.

| Scheduled approach | Availability approach |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| "Tuesday 7pm at the venue" | Being in London often enough that the text finds me available |
| "Must meet the neighbour this month" | Being in the building often enough that the staircase conversation happens |

**The formula**: Be available for twice as much opportunity as life will actually use.

### The Priority Shift: London Reframed

**Before this reflection**:
- London = work obligation
- Somerset = where life happens

**After this reflection**:
- London = community opportunity (close friends, neighbours, concerts, Toastmasters)
- Somerset = sanctuary
- When cutting back, **free London evenings first**

## The London Flat as Community Soil

The flat is not merely infrastructure serving the practice. It is *soil in which community grows*.

- Neighbours: found in the same building
- Access to concerts, Toastmasters, London cultural life
- Gateway to concentration of age-peers
- My wife can work remotely from London; shared rhythm possible

**Why NOT to move to more central London** (even if affordable): existing neighbours are sufficient reason. Community is place-specific and cannot be transplanted.

## Position on Gurus

The guru concept — submission not just to teaching but to the *person* — is fundamentally incompatible with my orientation. I will not be "sitting at the feet" of anyone. This is a "final" position.

**Implication**: Relationships with teachers will be collegial, not devotional. I can learn from teachers, feel reverence, be influenced by lineages — but autonomy is non-negotiable.
