## Contemplative Practice Design

**Viniyoga** (asana + pranayama): cultivates *stillness* — settling, quieting the mind, breath as organising principle.

**Burbea meditation**: cultivates something different — *acceptance, savouring, ways of looking*, investigation, the imaginal.

These are genuinely different capacities, not redundant. I will likely end up valuing both.

**Current architecture**:
- Viniyoga (asana + pranayama): daily foundation, ~50 mins
- Burbea-style mindfulness: additional layer, 2 × 20 mins morning and evening

### Constraints on Practice Design

- Must be compatible with autonomy (no guru submission)
- Must not claim exclusive truth (will trigger hostility)
- Must be collegial, not devotional; pragmatist, not dogmatic
- Must actually be done — the best practice is the one you'll stick with
