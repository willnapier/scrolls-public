## Pension & Investments

My pension (SIPP) is split between:
- A diversified portfolio managed by a financial advisor (steady growth)
- A significant concentrated high-conviction position — volatile

This is deliberate conviction, not accidental concentration. It is acceptable given: continued earning, parallel household wealth, property equity, and philosophical equanimity.

### Investment Philosophy

**Conventional wisdom**: At retirement, shift from growth to income.

**My consideration**: Leave assets in growth. Live off appreciation. Let the pot compound.

**Case for growth approach**:
- Multiple income sources (practice, household, eventually state pension)
- Not yet drawing down — still accumulating
- High-conviction thesis on concentrated position
- Parallel pension provides household redundancy

**Risk**: Sequence-of-returns — a major drawdown early in retirement plus withdrawals can permanently impair capital.

## Go-Go Years Spending Philosophy

Financial advisors emphasise spending more aggressively in one's 60s — the "Go-Go years" — when health and energy permit experiences that become harder to enjoy later.

### The Three Phases of Retirement Spending

| Phase | Age | Character | Spending Capacity |
|-------|-----|-----------|-------------------|
| **Go-Go** | 60-70 | Active, healthy, adventurous | **Highest** |
| **Slow-Go** | 70-80 | Still engaged but slowing | Moderate |
| **No-Go** | 80+ | Health limits activity | Lowest (often care costs) |

### The Insight

You cannot spend your way to the experiences of a 65-year-old when you're 80. The money becomes less useful as capacity declines.

### Escalating Withdrawal Strategy (Preferred Model)

Spend more each year through your 60s, when utility is highest. Accept a smaller (but still abundant) pension later. Annual withdrawals escalate year-on-year.

Under reasonable growth assumptions, even with aggressive spending through the Go-Go years, I arrive at 71 with a pension that is more than sufficient. The risk of spending aggressively is minimal. The risk of *not* spending is arriving with abundance but having deferred the experiences, generosity, and adventures that define the best decade.

**Conclusion**: Spend confidently in your 60s. The numbers support it.

## Risk Considerations

### Investment Concentration
Significant pension exposure to a single conviction position. Acceptable given multiple income sources, parallel household wealth, and philosophical orientation.

### Sequence-of-Returns Risk
Relevant if drawing down during market decline. Mitigated by continued income, multiple sources, and flexibility on withdrawal timing.

### Property Illiquidity
Property equity not quickly accessible. Mitigated by structured refinance strategy — no forced sale scenario exists.

### Health/Longevity
Not modelled explicitly. Working assumption: 25-35 years of active life from age 60.
